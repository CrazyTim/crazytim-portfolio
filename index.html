Job Scheduler, Buildsafe Australia
desktop app (ongoing)
vb.net,php,javascript,html,css,json,ms-sql,filemaker,sms,google-directions-api,gps,ui-design,dev-ops
I designed and have been the lead programmer for this in-house desktop application. It is used daily by 60+ office staff and allows us to prepare and schedule work across our 100+ vehicles and 13+ depots.
I am responsible for keeping it running smoothly (monitoring, debugging), and I work with stakeholders to help scope new features, implement, test, and deploy.
I have mainly focused on making it easy to use for non-technical staff. It is used by many departments including operations, drafting, auditing, factory, depot managers, finance, and training. In particular it integrates with Google Maps and GPS data, allows direct communication with customers via our website and SMS, and displays data from many disparate systems to help us make informed decisions.
Countdown
animation (2018)
webgl,three.js
A replication of the countdown animation from the film Mission to Mars. I love the design of this binary timer. The rings appear to be resting upon a liquid, which gives a sense of depth when they rotate. I had fun referencing the movie, and I learnt a lot about WebGL.
Interesting fact: 7 rings rotating at this speed lasts about a minute, which wasn't long enough to get through the scene in the movie!

Voyager Episode Archive
flash (2003)
prototype,audio,animation,data-collection,dat-entry,research,interface-design,audio
A functional Start Trek LCARS touch interface with sounds!
I examined many examples from the show to learn the design language. I wanted the design to be functional and meaningful, while at the same time fun to interact with.
Features include:
  • episode outline and full synopsis
  • category tags
  • stardate
  • star ratings
  • classification rating
  • and up to six photos per episode
  • Episodes can be navigated by season, topic, or chronological timeline
  • Search function and random episode selector
  • contextual help labels.
All sounds came from a Start Trek computer game. All the assets where bundled into a single flash file with no database :).
